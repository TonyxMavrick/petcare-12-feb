5 Signs Your Dog Needs More Exercise

Health & Wellness Dog Care

Is your furry friend getting enough physical activity? Many pet parents underestimate just how much exercise their dogs need to stay healthy and happy. Regular exercise isn't just about physical health—it's crucial for your dog's mental wellbeing too.

1. Destructive Behavior at Home

If your dog is chewing furniture, digging holes in the yard, or destroying shoes, they might have pent-up energy that needs an outlet. Dogs were bred to work, and without adequate exercise, they'll find their own (often destructive) ways to burn off energy. A tired dog is a good dog!

2. Weight Gain and Obesity

Just like humans, dogs need regular exercise to maintain a healthy weight. If you notice your pup packing on pounds despite a controlled diet, increased exercise might be the solution. Obesity in dogs can lead to serious health issues including joint problems, diabetes, and heart disease.

3. Hyperactivity and Restlessness

Does your dog pace around the house, unable to settle down? Excessive restlessness, especially during evening hours, often indicates that your dog hasn't had enough physical activity during the day. A proper exercise routine can help them relax and rest when it's time to wind down.

4. Attention-Seeking Behaviors

Constant barking, pawing, or nudging you for attention could be your dog's way of saying "I'm bored and need to move!" Dogs are social animals that crave interaction and stimulation. Regular walks and playtime provide both physical exercise and the mental engagement they need.

5. Decreased Muscle Tone

If your dog's muscles seem less defined or they appear to be losing their athletic build, it's a clear sign they need more activity. Regular exercise helps maintain muscle mass, improves cardiovascular health, and keeps joints flexible—especially important for aging dogs.

Creating a Healthy Exercise Routine

Start slowly if your dog isn't used to regular exercise, and gradually increase intensity and duration. Mix up activities to keep things interesting—try combining walks with fetch, swimming, or agility training. Always consider your dog's age, breed, and health condition when planning their exercise routine.

Remember, a well-exercised dog is not only healthier but also happier, calmer, and better behaved.
